## Part 1: Level 3 AI Changes Made

The new `runAILevel3(state)` function was added to `server.js` above the existing `runAI` function. The `scheduleAI` dispatcher was updated to call `runAILevel3` instead of `runAI`. The original `runAI` is preserved as a fallback/reference.

### New behaviors added (9 priority layers):

| Priority | Behavior | Description |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Threat Defense** | Detects enemy units within 2 hexes of any friendly city. Redirects nearby land units (within 4 hexes) to defend before any other action. |
| 2 | **Retreat When Losing** | If a land unit is isolated (no friendly units within 2 hexes) AND 2+ enemies are nearby, it retreats toward the nearest friendly city instead of advancing. |
| 3 | **Neutral City Capture** | Same as Level 2 — nearest available land unit is dispatched to each neutral city. |
| 4 | **City Defense (Baseline)** | Undefended friendly cities not already under threat get a garrison dispatched. |
| 5 | **Coordinated Squad Attacks** | Land units within 3 hexes of each other are grouped into squads. Each squad converges on the same objective: the enemy unit cluster with the most nearby allies, or the nearest enemy city. |
| 6 | **Naval Escort** | Transports wait for a destroyer or battleship to be within 1 hex before moving. If no escort is nearby, the AI moves the escort toward the transport first (transport sits still that turn). |
| 7 | **Anti-Air Awareness** | Fighters prioritize enemy bombers over other targets (bombers get a +1000 distance penalty to simulate urgency). Bombers avoid hexes where enemy fighters are adjacent — they scan for "safe targets" (enemy units not adjacent to a friendly fighter) and prefer those. |
| 8 | **Smart Production** | If the AI is losing cities (fewer than it started with), it builds army/tank at 60%/40% split. If it has more cities and a carrier, it builds fighters. Coastal cities without a transport build one first. If enemies have air units, counters with fighters. |
| 9 | **Remaining Land Units** | Same as Level 2 — advance toward nearest enemy city or neutral city. |

### Key differences from Level 2:
- Level 2 had a flat priority list; Level 3 evaluates threat state before committing units
- Level 2 had no squad coordination; Level 3 groups nearby units toward a shared objective
- Level 2 had no retreat logic; Level 3 retreats isolated units under threat
- Level 2 transport logic had no escort requirement; Level 3 transports wait for naval cover
- Level 2 air units attacked randomly; Level 3 fighters specifically hunt bombers, bombers avoid fighter cover
- Level 2 production was static; Level 3 adapts to whether the AI is winning or losing cities

## Part 2: Self-Play Simulation Results

The script (`selfplay.js`) was run multiple times. It creates a fresh 40x40 hex map, spawns both players symmetrically in opposing quadrants, then runs alternating turns of Level 3 AI for both sides with full production and fuel ticks.

### Key Design Finding: Landmass Separation

The map generator deliberately places players in **opposing quadrants** (P1: top-left, P2: bottom-right). On a 40x40 map with 70% ocean coverage, players frequently start on **separate islands** with distances of 15–31 hexes.

With army move speed = 1 hex/turn, reaching an enemy 25 hexes away takes 25+ turns (more with terrain), which in a 30-turn game means **no combat occurs at all**. The same-landmass flag was added to `selfplay.js` to force starting positions on the same landmass.

## Part 3: Bugs Found

### Bug 1: Army attack rate too low for effective ground combat
- **Description:** Armies have `attack: 1` (16.7% hit rate). In 30 turns, armies that do reach enemies usually just bounce off repeatedly. Most combat was incidental naval encounters.
- **Evidence:** Turn 31-33 of 60-turn run: P1 armies attacked P2 transport 6 times before hitting twice (4 misses, 2 hits). Attack=1 on d6 is too weak for the primary ground unit.
- **Classification:** Balance issue / design flaw.

### Bug 2: Transport-only production loop
- **Description:** In one run, P2 built 4 transports consecutively (no armies, no tanks). The production logic for coastal cities sets transport if `!aiHasTransport`, then battleship/destroyer if it does. But both cities were coastal and built alternately — the second city saw the first already building a transport and... also built a transport. This happened because city production is set once (when `!tile.city.production`), and they were both set at the same time before any transport was spawned.
- **Evidence:** selfplay_run1.txt: `Units produced by type: { "transport": 4 }` — zero combat units.
- **Classification:** Logic bug. The production check `aiHasTransport` should also check cities currently building a transport, not just spawned transports.

### Bug 3: Winner not declared when P1 has 0 cities but 1 unit for 20+ turns
- **Description:** In selfplay_run1.txt, P2 captured P1's only city on Turn 5. P1 had 1 unit but 0 cities for turns 5-25. `checkWin` requires BOTH 0 cities AND 0 units. P1's lone army roamed for 20 turns without being attacked (P2 had transports not armies). Eventually P1 recaptured a city on Turn 26.
- **Evidence:** Cities: P1=0, P2=2 for turns 5-25. P1 units remained at 1. No win declared for P2.
- **Classification:** This is actually correct per rules (you need to eliminate ALL enemy presence). However, **practically** it means a player with zero production can survive for many turns, creating very long endgames.

### Bug 4: BFS doesn't route AI through enemy-occupied tiles
- **Description:** The original `aiBFS` in `server.js` skips tiles with ≥2 units. For player 2, the check correctly identifies enemy tiles (`u.owner === 1`), but in `selfplay.js` the adapted BFS initially skipped all full tiles. After fixing, it still stops BFS expansion through enemy tiles (correct for non-air units), but the issue means the AI sometimes fails to find paths to enemy cities that are "defended" by parked units.
- **Evidence:** Transports being attacked repeatedly at the same hex (turns 31-35) — units were attacking but the path-of-attack was being rediscovered each turn.
- **Classification:** Minor/expected. The fix allows attack-routing to work but doesn't let the AI plan multi-step paths through enemy territory.

### Bug 5: Fuel crashes on bombers despite Level 3 bomber avoidance logic
- **Description:** 3 air unit crashes in the 30-turn run. Bombers were produced, flew toward enemy positions, then crashed on turn-end when not near a friendly city or carrier. Level 3 added bomber avoidance of fighter-covered hexes, but didn't add fuel-awareness (return to base when fuel low).
- **Evidence:** `Air unit crashes: 3` in the 30-turn primary run.
- **Classification:** Missing feature — fuel-aware navigation not implemented.

### Bug 6: Retreat logic never triggered (0 retreats across all runs)
- **Description:** The retreat condition requires: no friendly units within 2 hexes AND ≥2 enemies within 2 hexes. In practice, this combination rarely occurs because:
  1. Early game: units are spread out across a large map (few enemies nearby)
  2. Mid-game: units move toward objectives in groups (friendlies naturally cluster)
  3. Late game: winning player has overwhelming numbers (never isolated)
- **Evidence:** `Retreats triggered: 0` across all 4 simulation runs.
- **Classification:** Threshold tuning issue — the retreat trigger conditions are too strict. Loosening to "1 nearby enemy AND 0 friendlies within 1 hex" would trigger more often.

## Part 4: Suggestions for Game Improvements

### Critical Balance
1. **Increase army attack to 2** — armies need to be viable ground units. At attack=1 (16.7% hit), they're barely better than nothing. Increase to 2 (33%) to make ground combat feel meaningful.
2. **Reduce map size or max distance between starting cities** — a 40×40 map with 30-turn games means players often never meet. Either reduce to 25×25, or add a "max distance" cap in `generateMap` to ensure starting cities are ≤18 hexes apart.

### Production Logic
3. **Fix transport-only production loop** — check both spawned transports AND cities currently building transports before starting another: `const aiHasOrBuildingTransport = aiHasTransport || friendlyCities.some(fc => fc.city.production === 'transport')`.
4. **Add army as default fallback** — if no special condition matches and the city is not coastal, always build army (not bomber). Bombers without carriers crash within ~10 turns.

### AI Intelligence
5. **Add fuel-aware return-to-base for air units** — when a fighter or bomber has ≤3 fuel, override its target to the nearest friendly city or carrier. This eliminates unnecessary crashes.
6. **Loosen retreat trigger** — change the retreat threshold from "0 friendlies within 2 hexes AND ≥2 enemies within 2 hexes" to "0 friendlies within 1 hex AND ≥1 enemy within 2 hexes". This makes the feature actually activate.
7. **Squad coordination vs defense balance** — squads currently converge even when their home city is being threatened. The threat-defense priority runs first, but only redirects ONE unit per threatened city. Consider redirecting all nearby squad members to defense when a city is under immediate threat (enemy within 1 hex).

### Game Design
8. **Add turn limit/scoring** — 30 turns is too short for a 40×40 map. Add a 60-turn default, or add a scoring system (city count at turn limit = win condition) so games end meaningfully even without unit elimination.
9. **Fog of war for AI** — currently the AI has perfect information. Adding AI-specific visibility limits would make the game more interesting and prevent the AI from always directly pathfinding to the enemy's city.
10. **Naval unit production balance** — destroyers and battleships are expensive (6 and 14 build turns). By the time a battleship is ready, the game is often decided. Consider reducing battleship build time to 10 or adding a mid-tier naval unit (frigate, build time 8).
